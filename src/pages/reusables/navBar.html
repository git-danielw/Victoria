Victoria

Victoria is the smallest state in southeast Australia yet has the highest population density. Mountains, national parks, wineries, surfing beaches and diverse attractions are packed into this compact area.


Weather

Victoria's climate varies across the state. The north has much drier and warmer weather than the south. The climate is warm to hot in summer (December to February), mild in autumn (March to May), crisp in winter (June to August), and cool in spring (September to November).

  • Melbourne

    Melbourne is Victoria's capital which sits on the banks of the Yarra River and around the shores of Port Phillip Bay. It boasts art galleries, a packed program of festivals and events, stylish shopping, and a passion for eating and drinking.

  • Phillip Island

    Phillip Island is Victoria's holiday sweet spot. Home to the Little Penguins, idyllic beaches, captivating coastlines, unique wildlife, hi-octane motorsports, endless family fun and adventure awaits you on Phillip Island!

  • Geelong

    Geelong is a proud town with an interesting history and pockets of charm. It is located on Corio Bay, being the gateway to the famous Great Ocean Road.

  • Great Ocean Road

    One of Victoria's most scenic drives, the Great Ocean Road is a magnificent touring route from surf capital Torquay to the iconic 12 Apostles, ending at historic fishing village Port Fairy. Take the time to stop at the lookouts and watch out for wildlife!

Visas

How long are they valid for?

The visas are valid within 12 months of the date of issuance.

How much do they cost?

Prices start at AUD150. Fees vary, depending on the length of stay.

Processing times

  • 25% of applications: 16 Days
  • 50% of applications: 39 Days
  • 75% of applications: 63 Days
  • 90% of applications: 4 Months

Submit your application at least four months before you travel.

What’s the ETA?

The Electronic Travel Authority (ETA) visa application is completed online. No paperwork, or mailing or submitting your passport is required.

ETA visas are issued electronically - your passport is not stamped with a visa. When you apply online your information is collected electronically. The Australian Immigration authorities verify and confirm your authorization to travel and issue your visa electronically. At the airport when departing and upon arrival to Australia your passport is scanned and your ETA visa recognized allowing you entry.

Who’s eligible for ETA?

Passport holders from these countries are eligible for the ETA: the United States, Andorra, Austria, Belgium, Brunei, Canada, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hong Kong SAR, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Japan, Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, Malaysia, Malta, Monaco, the Netherlands / Holland, Norway, Portugal, Republic of San Marino, Singapore, South Korea, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Taiwan, United Kingdom, Vatican City.

Who is not eligible for an ETA? Who needs to use a regular print visa?

The ETA is not intended for:

  • Anyone not listed above.
  • Anyone planning to work in Australia.
  • Students planning to attend class or training for more than 3 months (90 days).
  • If you plan to attend class for over 1 month (4 weeks) and are 11 years of age or over.
  • If you have tuberculosis.
  • If you have any criminal convictions—this may bar your entry into the country.
